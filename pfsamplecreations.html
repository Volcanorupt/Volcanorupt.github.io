Box Lorry

Building Difficulty: Medium

Power Functions electronics used:

  • 1 Servo Motor
  • 1 L-Motor
  • 1 Light
  • 1 AA Battery Box
  • 1 IR Receiver

The L-Motor powers a central gear that turns the main axle that moves the 2 hind wheels. The Servo Motor powers a small gear that rotates on a gear rack, moving the front wheels left and right. The lights below the bumper will turn on when the battery box is turned on. It integrates many electronics in a small model.




Small Buggy

Building Difficulty: Easy

Power Functions electronics used:

  • 1 Servo Motor
  • 1 L-Motor
  • 1 AAA Battery Box
  • 1 IR Receiver

The L-Motor powers a gear-down system that trades overall torque for speed and moves the differential that moves the 2 hind wheels together. The Servo Motor powers a small gear that rotates on a gear rack, moving the front wheels left and right. The battery box and IR receiver had to be mounted on the side as there was no space on the chassis itself!



Grapple Truck

Building Difficulty: Hard

Power Functions electronics used:

  • 4 M-Motors
  • 1 Light
  • 1 AA Battery Box
  • 2 IR Receivers

Made by an avid Japanese Lego collector.

The 2 M-Motors power the left and right tracks to move independently. 1 M-Motor controls the height of the arm and the other M-Motor controls the pincers. It integrates many electronics in a small model.

Power Functions electronics used:

Made by an avid Japanese Lego collector.

The M-Motor powers a gear-up system that trades overall speed for torque and moves the differential that moves the 2 hind wheels together. The Servo Motor powers a small gear that moves the front wheels left and right.
